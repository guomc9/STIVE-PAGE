Shaping a Stabilized Video by Mitigating Unintended Changes for Text-Guided Video Editing

Code

Abstract

Text-guided video editing has experienced a surge in popularity; however, existing approaches often suffer from unintended alterations, where textual descriptions inadvertently affect other parts of the footage. To address this issue, this work aims to ensure that modifications are confined strictly to the targeted editing regions, thereby enhancing the consistency and naturalness of the generated videos. Specifically, we propose using concept augmented textual inversion, which significantly improves efficiency while maintaining plug-and-play characteristics during inference. Additionally, we stabilize text-driven video editing by tuning the video diffusion model with cross-attention supervision. Comparative evaluations against state-of-the-art methods demonstrate that our approach produces more stable and lifelike videos, both with and without the provision of concept videos.
